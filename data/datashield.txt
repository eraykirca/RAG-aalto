DataShield Act 2030
Preamble
--------
An Act to establish a unified federal framework governing Personal Data,
to create the Data Accountability Authority (DAA) and to harmonize state
privacy laws.

Definitions
-----------
- "Personal Data": information relating to an identified or identifiable
  natural person.  
- "Controller": entity determining purposes & means of processing.  
- "Processor": entity processing on behalf of a Controller.  
- "High-Risk Processing": profiling, large-scale biometrics, or automated
  decisions with legal effect ≥ \$5 k.

Individual Rights
-----------------
1. Right to Access (Art 7) — machine-readable export in 15 days.  
2. Right to Erasure (Art 8) — cascades to all downstream Processors.  
3. Right to Algorithmic Explanation (Art 9) — plain-language summary of
   high-risk models, including features & weight ranges.  
4. Right to Data Portability (Art 10) — direct API transfer to new service.

Controller Obligations
----------------------
- Data-Protection Officer if > 50 k records processed / year.  
- Data-Protection Impact Assessment (DPIA) for any dataset > 1 M records.  
- Annual third-party audit; summary published on DAA website.  
- Breach notification within 48 h.

Enforcement & Penalties
-----------------------
- Civil fines up to **4 %** of global revenue or **\$ 40 M**, whichever is
  higher.  
- Criminal liability (Class E felony) for willful re-identification.  
- DAA may issue corrective orders; repeated non-compliance → injunction.

Methodology (Implementation Roadmap)
-------------------------------------
I: Draft | 2026 Q2 | FTC, Senate Commerce | Public draft + RIS
II: Comment | 2026 Q3 | NGOs, Industry | Revised bill  
III: Pilot | 2027 | 2 volunteer states | Test DAA portal
IV: Full Enforcement | Jan 2030 | All | DAA operational

Technical Guidelines (DAA)
--------------------------
1. JSON-LD schema for DPIA submission.  
2. Standardised API (`/.well-known/datarights`) for access & erasure.  
3. Encryption at rest: AES-256-GCM; in transit: TLS 1.3.  
4. Differential-privacy noise for statistical releases (ε ≤ 1).

Example Scenario
----------------
A fintech uses facial-emotion analysis for loan approval. Under DataShield
it must:  
1. Conduct a DPIA.  
2. Provide opt-out for sensitive data.  
3. Supply algorithmic rationale (“emotion score < 0.3”).  
4. Offer manual review upon denial.

Compliance API Specification
----------------------------
Base URL: `https://api.daa.gov/v1`  
Auth: OAuth 2.1 JWT

Endpoints
---------
1. `POST /dpio` — upload DPIA JSON-LD (max 10 MB).  
2. `POST /erasure` — `{ subject_id, controller_id }`.  
3. `GET /status/{ticket}` — polling job status.

Citizen Guide (v1.0)
--------------------
Getting Started
---------------
1. Visit `myrights.daa.gov`.  
2. Enter email → verify code.  
3. Browse data-holding companies & request export.
